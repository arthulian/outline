# Cognitive Concepts - Visual Design Scheme

## 1. Design Philosophy

### Overall Visual Direction

- A sophisticated and focused aesthetic that prioritizes clarity and intellectual depth. The design draws inspiration from the minimalist interface design of *Wired* magazine and the principles of Swiss International Style, emphasizing structure and precision. The core idea is to create a focused, distraction-free environment where the complex information can be presented with the utmost clarity and elegance.

### Content Density

- High density. The content is structured and information-rich, suitable for a dashboard or a detailed knowledge base. The layout prioritizes clarity and efficient information consumption, displaying multiple key concepts at once in a structured, grid-like manner.

## 2. Color System

**Primary Colors**

- Background color: #0D1117 (Deep dark blue-gray, primary background establishing the dark mode foundation)
- Primary color: #58A6FF (Bright blue, for headings and key highlights)
- Secondary color: #8B949E (Medium gray, for supporting text)
- Accent color: #3FB950 (Green, for emphasis and interactive elements)

**Text Colors**

- Dark text (for light backgrounds): #0D1117
- Light text (for dark backgrounds): #C9D1D9

## 3. Font Design

**Heading Fonts**

- English fonts
  - Chiron Hei HK (A modern geometric sans-serif with sharp lines, providing a strong technological and precise feel for titles)

**Body Fonts**

- English fonts
  - Quattrocento Sans (A classic and highly readable sans-serif, chosen for its clarity and comfortable reading experience in dense text blocks)

## 4. Image Style

- Minimal and abstract. Use subtle, abstract geometric patterns or data visualizations as background elements. Avoid photographic imagery to maintain a focused, conceptual atmosphere. The design should be text-driven, with visual elements serving to structure and highlight information.
