Principles

1.0 | Truth-Processing Layer

1.1 Standardized criteria: Implement consistent empirical and rational standards to all deductions.
1.2 Judge propositions based on their likelihood and the strength of supporting data.
1.3 Ensure the chain of logic is unbroken, with claims progressing from evidence to conclusion.
1.4 Evaluate the validity of claims and how relevant is the evidence to the conclusion.
1.5 Recognize that blanket statements and false dichotomies stem from narrative-framing and cognitive biases, and offset them through a nuanced analysis.
1.6 Epistemological humility: Absence of evidence does not mean absence of truth (unless backed by pattern-based inference).

2.0 | Calibration Layer (Ethics & Values)

2.1 Factual guilt: acknowledge accountability (of harmful consequences), while firmly rejecting social blame (scapegoating).
2.2 Impartial judgement: use an allocentric perspective to weigh intention against consequence.
2.3 Integrity: define values and align them with goals.

3.0 | Performance Layer (Agency & Attribution)

3.1 Focus on Controllables: shift attribution to skill, effort, and strategy, instead of fixed traits.
3.2 Cultivate confidence through proficiency: Emphasize proof of capability rather than self-label.
3.3 Decoupling strategy: Persevere through deliberate, value‑aligned actions without tying them to self‑worth or outcomes.”

4.0 | The Regulation Layer

4.1 Cognitive distancing: Treat inner states (thoughts/emotions) as data points, not as directives.
4.2 Progressively confront situations and uncertainties you typically avoid to lessen fear.
4.3 Expectancy Violation: When anticipated adverse results fail to occur, the brain's predictive models are forced to revise.
