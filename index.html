Framework

Section 1 · Intellectual & Epistemic Humility

A. Core Principles
ItemDescription
Epistemic Pre-commitment(a) Define falsifiers before forming conclusions; (b) Avoid changing standards that move the goalposts; (c) Focus on getting it right rather than being right.
Awareness of Knowledge LimitsRecognize what you don’t know and challenge assumptions with evidence.
Testing Argument BoundariesIdentify weak points and test reasoning under scrutiny.
B. Stress-Testing Methods
MethodDescription
Mirror TestAccurately represent opposing arguments.
Graded ConfidenceScale belief strength with evidential support.

Section 2 · Equanimity Through Self-Control

A. Observer Mindset
ConceptDescription
Observer MindsetNotice internal states without becoming entangled.
B. Effort Allocation
Control LevelDescription
ControllableApply full effort where actions affect outcomes.
Partial ControlScale effort to realistic influence.
UncontrollableRefrain from effort to prevent wasted energy.
C. Internal Validation
ConceptDescription
Volition-based GroundingAnchor your self-worth in your efforts, values, and authenticity, rather than external validation or results.

Section 3 · Resilience Through Stress Inoculation

A. Stress Response Profiles
ResponseDescription
DistressDefend system integrity under threat.
EustressUse challenge to improve structure.
B. Hormesis
ConceptDescription
The Goldilocks ZoneModerate challenge with periods of recovery drives growth.
C. Self-Efficacy Cycle
ConceptDescription
Confidence BuildingConfidence emerges from adaptive success.
Caution
Abandoning agency undermines adaptation and risks learned helplessness. When in doubt, Section 3 should override the “Refrain from Effort” rule in Section 2.
