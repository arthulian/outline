MINDSET STANDARDS

ACCURACY BEFORE EGO

Prioritize being correct over being "right" or feeling correct

Systematic
Skepticism

Maintain systematic skepticism of your own reasoning to avoid bias and blind spots.

Identify Falsifiers

Identify potential falsifiers before committing to conclusions.

Objective Evaluation

Use objective evaluation; avoid motivated reasoning fueled by ego-based desires.

STRESS-TESTING METHODS

Steelman + Mirror Test

Accurately reconstruct opposing arguments, then evaluate whether they meet their own standard for truth.

Confidence Calibration

Align certainty with quality of evidence. High confidence requires strong evidence; update proportionally to new data.

INTERNAL-STATE REGULATION

Disentangling Identity

Observe thoughts and emotions as data without conflating them with your identity. You are not your thoughts; you are the observer of them.

EFFORT ALLOCATION

CONTROLLABLE

Apply maximum effort where actions directly affect outcomes

PARTIALLY CONTROLLABLE

Adjust effort based on level of influence

UNCONTROLLABLE

Do not expend energy; redirect toward controllable factors

SELF-ESTEEM ANCHORS

✓ SECURE

Self-worth grounded in positive self-regard rooted in healthy relationships with mutual respect, trust, emotional regulation, self-disclosure, and effective conflict repair.

✗ ANXIOUS

Self-worth dependent on external validation or achievement

✗ AVOIDANT

Self-worth tied to performative self-reliance

ADAPTIVE RESILIENCE

STRESS TYPES

■ DISTRESS (Sympathetic Overdrive)

Reduce triggers, step back, return to baseline. Chronic activation harms health and decision quality.

✦ OPTIMAL STRESS (Goldilocks Zone)

Channel into productive effort. The inverted-U curve where moderate stress enhances focus and performance.

SELF-ESTEEM CYCLE

1. ACTION

Build competence through deliberate practice

2. INTERPRETATION

Attribute outcomes to effort and strategy (not luck or fixed traits)

3. CONFIDENCE

Update self-efficacy based on evidence, not emotion

4. EXPANSION

Increased capability enables harder challenges

■ This virtuous cycle compounds: small wins → evidence-based confidence → greater challenges → mastery

KEY INSIGHT: Resilience is not bouncing back — it is growing forward through calibrated effort, accurate self-appraisal, and deliberate skill-building.

INTEGRATION & DAILY PRACTICE

Putting Humility-Based Integrity into Practice

MINDSET IN ACTION

Before concluding, ask: What would falsify this? Am I steelmanning the other side? Is my confidence proportional to evidence?

REGULATION IN ACTION

Label the emotion as data. Sort effort by controllability. Anchor self-worth in secure relationships, not outcomes or performance.

RESILIENCE IN ACTION

Convert distress to optimal stress. Run the self-esteem cycle deliberately. Treat setbacks as data for the next iteration.

"Humility is not thinking less of yourself — it is thinking of yourself less, while seeing yourself more accurately."
