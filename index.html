Principles

A Four-Layer Cognitive Framework

1.0 Truth-Processing Layer

  • Ensure logical coherence with each claim progressing from evidence to conclusion.
  • Consistently evaluate empirical and rational criteria across all deductions.
  • Evaluate the validity of claims by assessing the relevance and justification of their supporting evidence.
  • Evaluate propositions based on their propability and the strength of the supporting data.
  • Test for falsifiability: Framing might cause false dichotomies, which encourage overgeneralizations.
  • Epistemological humility: Absence of evidence does not imply absence of truth (unless backed by Sufficient Reason).

2.0 Calibration Layer (Ethics & Values)

  • Accept guilt and accountability for harmful consequences while rejecting being the target of blame (scapegoating).
  • Impartial judgement: use perspective-taking to weigh intention against consequence.
  • Integrity: define values and align them with goals.

3.0 Performance Layer (Agency & Attribution)

  • Locus of control: Attribute outcomes to controllable factors (skill, effort, and strategy); instead of fixed personal traits.
  • Skill-based confidence: Emphasize proof of capability rather than self-description.
  • Decouple self-worth from outcomes and focus on value‑aligned actions.

4.0 The Regulation Layer

  • Cognitive distancing: Treat thoughts and emotions as data points, not as directives.
  • Progressively confront uncertainties you typically avoid to reduce fear.
  • Use "expectancy violation" to update the brain's predictive models when outcomes differ from expectations.
